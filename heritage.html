Heritage



My Dad's Family

I come from a diverse family, with roots primarily in Mexican and Irish culture. I am only a quarter Mexican and half Irish. My father is half Mexican and half Irish. His mother, my abuelita, was the daughter of Mexican immigrants, specifically from the Guadalajara region. My abuelita was one of 17 and resided on the south east side of Chicago. Her family, The Castro Family, had an exhibit at the Mexican Museum of Art at one point.



On the other side, my dad’s dad, my poppy, was the son of Irish immigrants. My poppy’s father, my great-grandfather, was Tommie Ryan. He was an Irishman who was part of the IRA and labeled as a terrorist under the British. He blew up bridges and knocked down trees to support the Irish against the Brits. He was arrested, and at a jail camp, he became close with the soon to be Prime Minister of Irealnd. He was then kicked out of the country after a failed escape attempt. On the ship to America, he met his soon to be wife. They settled on the west side of Chicago where Tommie Ryan became an Irish Political Activist. He started the Shannon Rovers Irish Bagpipe Band and became close with Richard Daley. Wherever Richard Daley went, the Shannon Rovers played. Tommie Ryan also started the St. Patrick’s parade downtown, where my great uncle still announces it.



My Mama's Family

Now onto my mama’s parents. My mama’s mother, my grandma, is the daughter of European immigrants, one of which was Jewish. They resided on the West Side, in Austin. My grandma and her sisters actually all attended Austin Highschool. My mama’s father, my grandpa, has an established lineage in Alabama where he was born. He is of both Scotch Irish and Native American heritage.
